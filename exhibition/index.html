Digital Pioneers: The Genesis of Net Art

Curated by Jin Zhong

Curatorial Statement

The mid-1990s marked a transformative period in art history, as the internet emerged as a novel medium for creative expression. This exhibition, "Digital Pioneers: The Genesis of Net Art," delves into the groundbreaking works that defined the early days of net art. By featuring seminal pieces from platforms such as Alt-X, Art Teleportacia, and bram.org, we explore how artists harnessed the web's potential to challenge traditional art forms, foster interactive experiences, and critique digital culture. These creations not only expanded the boundaries of artistic expression but also laid the groundwork for contemporary digital practices. Through this collection, we invite viewers to reflect on the transformative power of the internet and its enduring impact on art.

Artworks

"Grammatron" (1997) by Mark Amerika

Hosted on Alt-X, "Grammatron" is an early hypertext narrative that immerses users in a nonlinear story, exemplifying the fusion of literature and digital media. Readers navigate through interconnected text and multimedia elements, experiencing a groundbreaking form of digital storytelling.

"My Boyfriend Came Back from the War" (1996) by Olia Lialina

Featured on Art Teleportacia, this interactive web narrative employs frames and hyperlinks to depict a fragmented love story. Users engage in a unique storytelling experience that reflects on communication and memory, showcasing the potential of the web as a medium for narrative art.

View Artwork

"Being Human" (1997) by Annie Abrahams

Presented on bram.org, "Being Human" is an interactive piece that invites users to explore human emotions and relationships through a series of web pages. It challenges the boundaries between artist and audience, emphasizing the participatory nature of net art and the internet's role in facilitating new forms of interaction.
